Linear Solvers¶

In this Chapter we give an overview of the linear solvers in AMReX. AMReX supports two solvers where the solution, \(\phi\) is either cell-centered or nodal. For each case you can solve on a single level (using essentially Dirichlet conditions supplied from coarser levels or physical boundary conditions), or you can do a multilevel composite solve on a subset of AMR levels (or even all the AMR levels).

The tutorial amrex/Tutorials/LinearSolvers/ABecLaplacian_C shows how to call the cell-centered solver. The tutorial amrex/Tutorials/Basic/HeatEquation_EX3_C shows how to solve the implicit heat equation using the cell-centered solver. Here an “ABecLaplacian” has the form

\[(a_{coeff} A - b_{coeff} \nabla \cdot B \nabla ) \phi = RHS\]

where \(a_{coeff}\), \(b_{coeff}\) are scalars, \(A\), \(B\), and \(RHS\) are MultiFabs. For the cell-centered solver, \(A\) and \(RHS\) are cell centered, and \(B\) is an array of face-centered MultiFabs since \(B \nabla \phi\) lives on faces, and the divergence lives on cell-centers.
